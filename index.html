The challenge

Growing businesses outgrow spreadsheets and ad‑hoc systems.

As operations scale, complexity compounds. Manual tools and generic CRMs are not designed to handle the operational and financial realities of multi-location, service-driven organizations.

Operational chaos

Staff rosters, customer lists, programs, and events sit in disconnected documents. Each center maintains its own version of the truth, making it difficult for owners to see what is actually happening.

Manual payment tracking

Cash books, screenshots, and exports from payment gateways are reconciled manually. End‑of‑day and end‑of‑month checks are slow, error‑prone, and dependent on specific staff.

Revenue leakage

Untracked refunds, missed renewals, and inconsistent discounts quietly erode margins. Without structured transaction histories, revenue leakage is hard to identify and quantify.

Staff mismanagement

Roles and permissions are unclear. Staff often have broader access than necessary, while owners lack visibility into who is performing which actions in the system.

Limited financial visibility

Owners see fragments of financial performance from different locations and systems. It is difficult to answer simple questions like “What did we actually collect this month?” with confidence.

No single source of truth

Customers, memberships, events, and fees are managed in separate tools. There is no unified, owner‑grade system that connects operations, finance, tax, and payouts.

Feature breakdown

Owner‑grade modules, designed for operations-heavy businesses.

Operations Control

Standardize how staff, customers, slots, and memberships are configured and managed across centers.

Staff Management

Add, edit, and activate/deactivate staff with clearly defined roles and permissions.

Prevent over‑permissioned access, simplify onboarding and offboarding, and keep operational ownership with the business—not individual employees.

Business impact: Lower operational risk, fewer errors, and clear accountability.

Example: A coaching chain assigns owners, finance controllers, and center managers distinct access scopes, ensuring only authorized users can change fees, tax settings, or payouts.

Customer Management

Maintain a centralized customer database with status tracking (Active, Pending) and join dates.

Replace fragmented lists with a single, reliable view of every learner and guardian across locations.

Business impact: Improved retention tracking, cleaner reporting, and more effective follow‑ups.

Example: A training institute segments all customers who joined in the last 30 days and coordinates a structured onboarding sequence across centers.

Slot & Membership Management

Configure structured programs, slots, and packages with titles, descriptions, and frequency bundles (e.g., 3 hours/week, 4x/week).

Align what is sold commercially with how classes are actually delivered, ensuring operational and financial consistency.

Business impact: Faster rollout of new offerings and fewer discrepancies between what was sold and what is scheduled.

Example: A coding academy creates standardized weekend bootcamp bundles and publishes them centrally so every branch sells the same defined packages.

Financial Intelligence

Gain a transaction‑level view of collections, cash approvals, royalties, and payouts.

Transactions

Record every payment with method (Cash / Online), status (Pending, Succeeded, Refunded), and sync state.

Consolidate all revenue data in one system and filter by program, event, status, or center.

Business impact: Faster month‑end close and more reliable revenue reporting.

Example: An owner filters all refunded transactions for the last quarter to quantify revenue loss by program.

Cash Approvals

Introduce a manual validation workflow where designated users approve or reject cash entries.

Cash is a high‑risk area; approvals ensure that recorded amounts match physical collections.

Business impact: Reduced cash leakage and stronger internal controls.

Example: At day end, each center submits cash totals; a finance controller reviews and approves them before they appear in final revenue reports.

Payout & Royalty Tracking

Summarize monthly royalties, platform fees, fixed fees, and total payable amounts per center or franchise.

Provide transparent, repeatable calculations for all stakeholders in franchise or revenue‑share models.

Business impact: Fewer disputes, faster settlements, and healthier partner relationships.

Example: A franchisor generates monthly royalty statements for all centers and records payouts directly in the portal.

Tax & Governance

Keep tax policies, permissions, and publishing behavior aligned with your operating model.

Tax Policies

Define tax rules with percentages, activation dates, and status controls for each jurisdiction.

Ensure programs, memberships, and events apply correct tax behavior consistently.

Business impact: Reduced compliance risk and simpler collaboration with accountants.

Example: When a tax rate changes, the owner updates it once in the portal and future transactions follow the new rules.

Role‑based Access

Configure granular permissions for owners, finance, managers, and staff across modules.

Restrict access to sensitive data and actions while keeping local teams productive.

Business impact: Clear separation of duties and reduced operational risk.

Example: Only finance roles can change payout settings, while center managers can manage customers and local events.

Status Control

Use Draft, Active, Inactive, and Publish states across programs, events, and tax policies.

Separate configuration work from live operations so incomplete setups never reach customers.

Business impact: Fewer configuration errors and safer rollouts.

Example: New membership bundles remain in Draft until pricing and tax are reviewed, then go Live with a single status change.

Growth & Engagement

Use events and structured support to drive enrollment and protect customer experience.

Events Management

Align campaigns, workshops, and bootcamps across centers while keeping owners in control.

Business impact: Consistent event quality and clearer attribution of event‑driven revenue.

Support Ticketing

Track customer issues with categories, priorities, statuses, and timestamps in a structured queue.

Move sensitive interactions out of ad‑hoc channels and into a managed, trackable system.

Business impact: Faster resolutions and better visibility into recurring issues.

Example: Refund‑related tickets are categorized and reviewed regularly to identify process gaps.

Program Memberships

Manage multiple membership types with pricing, tax behavior (inclusive/exclusive), and publish controls.

Align financial models with the way your programs actually operate.

Business impact: More predictable revenue and fewer pricing inconsistencies across centers.

Example: A franchise sets different membership prices for two regions while keeping fee structure and tax logic consistent.
